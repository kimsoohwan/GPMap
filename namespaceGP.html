class  DerivativeTrainingData
 Functional and derivative training data.

Note that it has two kinds of training inputs and single training outputs as follows.

Training Data Description
\(\mathbf{X} \in \mathbb{R}^{N \times D}\) Training inputs of functional observations
\(\mathbf{X}_d \in \mathbb{R}^{N_d \times D}}\) Training inputs of derivative observations
\(\mathbf{yy}_d \in \mathbb{R}^{N + N_d \times D}\) Training outputs of both functional and derivative observations

or

\[ \{\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{X}_d, \mathbf{yy}_d\} = \{(\mathbf{X}, \mathbf{y})_i\}_{i=1}^N \cup \left\{\left(\mathbf{X}_d, \frac{dy}{d\mathbf{x}_1}, ..., \frac{dy}{d\mathbf{x}_D}\right)_i\right\}_{i=1}^{N_d}, \]

where
\(N\): number of function observations
\(N_d\): number of derivative observations
\(D\): number of dimensions

This is for making it simple to retrieve the training outputs; if functional and derivative outputs are stored in separate variables, they need to be concatenated when retrieved from mean functions.

Note that the order of training outputs is

\begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{y}\\ \frac{dy}{d\mathbf{x}_1}\\ \vdots\\ \frac{dy}{d\mathbf{x}_D}\\ \end{bmatrix}

For example, suppose that you have a set of points, and some part of them have surface normals. Then, the training inputs are devided into two parts, functional and derivative observations, while the traing outputs are combined into one. This is why DerivativeTrainingData inherits from TrainingData but has its own derivative inputs.

Also note that the functional inputs and derivative inputs can be the same. More...
